# Information-Efficiency Method

Each news item is evaluated using publication/first-seen time, EUR/USD relevance, semantic novelty, duplication, source diversity, surprise, age and price-absorption proxies. A residual information-value prototype multiplies available normalized novelty, relevance, surprise, non-absorption and data quality.

States are NEW INFORMATION, PARTIALLY ABSORBED, MOSTLY ABSORBED, FULLY ABSORBED or INSUFFICIENT EVIDENCE. The score does not imply a profitable edge. Later market movement is not assigned to news that was not yet available.
