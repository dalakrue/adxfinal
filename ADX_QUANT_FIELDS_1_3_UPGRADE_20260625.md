# ADX Quant Pro — Fields 1–3 Research and Upgrade Report

**Date:** 25 June 2026  
**Primary instrument/timeframe:** EURUSD H1  
**Deployment entry point preserved:** `app.py`  
**Protection rule:** Existing production decision, Field 1 source-of-truth, Power BI central path, regime formulas, KNN/Greedy/NLP logic, thresholds, and canonical publication identity were not replaced. New behavior is wiring, caching, rendering, daily publication locking, and additive shadow calibration.

## 1. What was repaired

### Quick Run Fields 1–3 + Open Lunch

The Quick button now has a strict Fields 1–3 execution scope. It skips live NLP/research, Similar-Day Field 4, history research, ten-paper research, Quant V3/V4/V6/V7/V8, Field 9, and the Field 6–9 AI publication layer. A same-completed-H1 reuse guard returns the already-published immutable generation when the source signature and Field 1/2/3 caches are unchanged. After the calculation transaction completes, navigation opens Lunch on Field 1 and stops; opening Lunch does not start another calculation.

This architecture reduces avoidable work but does **not** claim a fixed percentage improvement without a live before/after profile on the user's API, machine, broker feed, and history size. The largest expected reductions occur when the same completed H1 candle is rerun or when the previous Quick path was invoking Full-only research.

### Field 2 Power BI renderer

The visible cached renderer now imports the dependencies it actually uses:

- `metric_text`
- `normalize_scalar`
- `numpy as np`

This removes the reported `NameError: name 'metric_text' is not defined`. The existing read-only saved-path recovery remains intact: when the visible cached bundle is sparse, it may recover a valid current-generation central path without recalculating or mutating the protected Power BI cache.

### Shared FX Session Selector

Fields 1 and 2 now use one shared session contract:

- **Auto** derives the session from the newest completed canonical/API candle time.
- **Manual** supports Asia/Sydney, London, London–New York overlap, New York, and global fallback.
- Internal codes and human-readable labels are normalized consistently.
- Field 1 shows the selected/detected session and current session evidence.
- Field 2 retains the protected central path and adds a bounded session-adjusted shadow path.

The session adjustment is leakage-safe. It uses completed H1 candles only, settled forecast residuals when available, shrinkage toward global evidence, and a maximum correction of ±0.35 ATR. Manual session changes therefore change the additive session path, while the protected base path remains auditable and unchanged.

### Field 3 daily-locked Middle/Higher regimes

The existing regime analytics are reused. A separate publication lock now governs the visible multi-standard regime cards:

- **Lower standard:** rolling, approximately 24 H1 candles.
- **Middle standard:** 120 completed H1 candles; assessed at broker-day 00:00 and locked until the next broker day.
- **Higher standard:** 600 completed H1 candles; assessed at broker-day 00:00 and locked until the next broker day.

An intraday diagnostic candidate may change, but it does not overwrite the locked Middle/Higher publication. Field 3 now shows regime start, next review, hours remaining, transition risk, alpha/delta diagnostics, and the exact raw sample count. This prevents the displayed 5-day and 25-day standards from flipping every few hours while preserving the underlying production logic.

### Copy Short and Copy Full

Every visible Lunch/menu copy control now uses a new current-generation serializer:

- Includes only current Fields 1–3 facts needed for a decision.
- Excludes 25-day history tables and DataFrames.
- Excludes stale generations.
- Excludes `Unavailable`, missing, failed-safely, empty, and placeholder values.
- Copy Short is compact; Copy Full is a fuller current snapshot, not a history export.
- Existing legacy history/export serializers remain available internally for compatibility and were not deleted.

## 2. Ten research papers and how to apply them

> Important: A paper supplies a model, test, or theorem under assumptions. It does not guarantee profitable trading, correct regimes, or future accuracy. Each addition should remain shadow-only until walk-forward evidence proves benefit.

### Paper 1 — James D. Hamilton (1989)

**Exact title:** *A New Approach to the Economic Analysis of Nonstationary Time Series and the Business Cycle*  
**Publication:** Econometrica, 57(2), 357–384.  
**Stable reference:** https://www.jstor.org/stable/1912559

**Concept:** Parameters of a time-series model can depend on an unobserved discrete state, with state changes governed by a Markov chain.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** EURUSD H1 behavior is not generated by one stationary process. Trend, compression, transition, and high-volatility states have different conditional distributions.

**Core principle:** For latent regime \(S_t\), the transition probability is

\[
P(S_t=j\mid S_{t-1}=i)=p_{ij},
\]

and filtering updates \(P(S_t=j\mid y_{1:t})\) from the previous state probabilities and the likelihood of the newest observation.

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Keep the existing named regime as production truth. Add a shadow posterior from returns, ATR-normalized range, realized volatility, alpha, delta, and direction persistence. Publish a regime change only when posterior evidence and the existing detector agree or when a validated transition threshold is crossed.

**Benefit:** More stable regime probabilities, explicit transition uncertainty, and fewer label flips caused by one H1 bar.

**Efficient implementation:** Two to five states, diagonal/sparse transition matrix, incremental filtering, no full-history refit during Streamlit reruns.

### Paper 2 — Andrew J. Filardo (1994)

**Exact title:** *Business-Cycle Phases and Their Transitional Dynamics*  
**Publication:** Journal of Business & Economic Statistics, 12(3), 299–308.  
**DOI:** 10.1080/07350015.1994.10524545

**Concept:** Markov transition probabilities can vary with observable information rather than remain constant.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** EURUSD regime persistence depends on session, volatility, day boundary, macro-event proximity, and current transition pressure.

**Core principle:** Replace constant \(p_{ij}\) with a logistic function:

\[
p_{ij,t}=\frac{\exp(x_t'\beta_{ij})}{\sum_k\exp(x_t'\beta_{ik})}.
\]

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Use selected/detected FX session, ATR percentile, delta acceleration, spread/data quality, and broker-day boundary as transition covariates. Do not let this shadow layer replace the current regime until it passes conditional walk-forward tests.

**Benefit:** London/NY transitions can receive different probabilities from Asia/Sydney instead of forcing one global transition rule.

**Efficient implementation:** Evaluate transition logits only once per completed H1 candle; cache coefficients and input vector.

### Paper 3 — Torben G. Andersen and Tim Bollerslev (1997)

**Exact title:** *Intraday Periodicity and Volatility Persistence in Financial Markets*  
**Publication:** Journal of Empirical Finance, 4(2–3), 115–158.  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S0927-5398(97)00004-2

**Concept:** High-frequency market volatility has strong periodic time-of-day structure. Ignoring it can distort estimates of persistence and forecast error.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** The same EURUSD H1 signal has different expected range, liquidity, and error behavior in Asia/Sydney, London, overlap, and New York.

**Core principle:** Decompose volatility into a periodic component and a stochastic component rather than treating all hours as identically distributed.

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Build session-conditioned residual and direction-accuracy tables from completed forecasts. Shrink each session estimate toward the global estimate:

\[
\hat\theta_s^{shrunk}=w_s\hat\theta_s+(1-w_s)\hat\theta_{global},\quad w_s=\frac{n_s}{n_s+k}.
\]

This is the basis of the implemented shared selector and bounded Field 2 session shadow path.

**Benefit:** The path can respond differently to London and New York without corrupting the protected central prediction.

**Efficient implementation:** One vectorized classification over a bounded 2,400-row H1 frame; no per-session DataFrame copies.

### Paper 4 — Fulvio Corsi (2009)

**Exact title:** *A Simple Approximate Long-Memory Model of Realized Volatility*  
**Publication:** Journal of Financial Econometrics, 7(2), 174–196.  
**DOI:** 10.1093/jjfinec/nbp001

**Concept:** HAR-RV represents persistent volatility using a parsimonious additive cascade of short-, medium-, and long-horizon realized volatility components.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** Short, medium, and longer-horizon participants jointly influence EURUSD volatility; one lookback is insufficient.

**Core principle:** A typical HAR form is

\[
RV_{t+1}=\beta_0+\beta_d RV_t^{(d)}+\beta_w RV_t^{(w)}+\beta_m RV_t^{(m)}+\varepsilon_{t+1}.
\]

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Align the three standards to completed-H1 windows: 24, 120, and 600 candles. Use their realized range/volatility as regime evidence and uncertainty scaling—not as a replacement for Field 1 decisions.

**Benefit:** Better separation of temporary H1 noise from persistent 5-day and 25-day state.

**Efficient implementation:** Maintain rolling sums/variance accumulators; update in O(1) per new candle instead of rescanning all windows on every UI rerun.

### Paper 5 — Ryan Prescott Adams and David J. C. MacKay (2007)

**Exact title:** *Bayesian Online Changepoint Detection*  
**Reference:** arXiv:0710.3742.  
**Stable reference:** https://arxiv.org/abs/0710.3742

**Concept:** Maintain an online posterior over run length—the number of observations since the most recent change point.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** A regime should not change merely because a rolling label changes; a genuine change should have measurable posterior evidence.

**Core principle:** Recursively update \(P(r_t\mid x_{1:t})\) using a hazard function, growth probabilities, and changepoint probabilities.

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Feed ATR-normalized returns, range, alpha/delta, and residual shift into a low-cost BOCPD shadow detector. Use the changepoint probability as Field 3 “transition risk.” Middle/Higher standards remain locked for the broker day, while a high changepoint probability warns that the next 00:00 review may switch.

**Benefit:** Separates “possible transition” from “confirmed regime,” making the UI more reliable.

**Efficient implementation:** Truncate run-length support (for example 120 or 600), prune negligible posterior mass, update only on a completed H1 candle.

### Paper 6 — Isaac Gibbs and Emmanuel Candès (2021)

**Exact title:** *Adaptive Conformal Inference Under Distribution Shift*  
**Publication:** NeurIPS 2021, volume 34, 1660–1672.  
**Reference:** arXiv:2106.00170.

**Concept:** Adapt prediction-set calibration online when the data distribution shifts.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** Fixed Power BI bands will under-cover in some sessions/regimes and over-cover in others.

**Core principle:** Update a calibration level using recent coverage error so long-run coverage tracks the target, even under changing distributions.

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Keep the central path unchanged. Calibrate lower/upper bands from settled errors by horizon and, only with enough samples, by session/regime. Apply finite-sample fallback to global residuals.

**Benefit:** More honest uncertainty bands and fewer false impressions of precision during London/NY volatility changes.

**Efficient implementation:** Store only rolling nonconformity scores/quantiles per horizon/session; avoid retaining full prediction objects in session state.

### Paper 7 — Raffaella Giacomini and Halbert White (2006)

**Exact title:** *Tests of Conditional Predictive Ability*  
**Publication:** Econometrica, 74(6), 1545–1578.  
**DOI:** 10.1111/j.1468-0262.2006.00718.x

**Concept:** Forecast comparison should be conditional on the information available at forecast time, especially when models are misspecified.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** “Best overall” does not imply “best in London” or “best in a high-transition regime.”

**Core principle:** Test whether conditional expected loss differences are zero:

\[
E[h_t\,\Delta L_{t+1}]=0,
\]

where \(h_t\) contains state/session information available at forecast origin.

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Compare base versus session-adjusted path using settled 1h/3h/6h loss, conditioned on FX session, regime, and transition risk. Only promote a session adjustment if it improves out-of-sample conditional loss with adequate observations.

**Benefit:** Directly targets the user's complaint that one path works in Asia/Sydney but fails in London/NY.

**Efficient implementation:** Maintain sufficient statistics per condition instead of repeatedly rebuilding all 25-day tables.

### Paper 8 — Peter R. Hansen (2005)

**Exact title:** *A Test for Superior Predictive Ability*  
**Publication:** Journal of Business & Economic Statistics, 23(4), 365–380.  
**DOI:** 10.1198/073500105000000063

**Concept:** SPA tests whether any candidate forecast is genuinely better than a benchmark while reducing the influence of poor or irrelevant alternatives.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** Trying many session weights, thresholds, and regime features can create a false “winner.”

**Core principle:** Use studentized loss differentials and a sample-dependent null distribution, commonly with bootstrap inference.

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Benchmark every shadow variant against the protected central path. Evaluate direction loss, MAE, interval score, and decision utility across walk-forward folds. Do not promote based on one best backtest row.

**Benefit:** Prevents accidental degradation from over-tuning London/NY corrections.

**Efficient implementation:** Run SPA offline or in Full Research mode only; Quick mode reads the latest validated result.

### Paper 9 — David H. Bailey, Jonathan M. Borwein, Marcos López de Prado, and Qiji Jim Zhu

**Exact title:** *The Probability of Backtest Overfitting*  
**Publication:** Journal of Computational Finance, 20(4).  
**DOI:** 10.21314/JCF.2016.322

**Concept:** Combinatorially Symmetric Cross-Validation (CSCV) estimates the probability that the selected best backtest is overfit.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** More tested configurations increase the chance that apparent improvement is selection noise.

**Core principle:** Repeatedly split performance paths into complementary in-sample/out-of-sample combinations, select the in-sample winner, and measure its out-of-sample rank degradation.

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Track every attempted session correction, regime threshold, and band calibration as a trial. Estimate PBO before promoting any configuration. Preserve chronological blocks and do not randomly shuffle H1 observations.

**Benefit:** Makes research governance stronger and protects production logic from “best-looking” but fragile changes.

**Efficient implementation:** Full/Research mode only, with cached fold results and compact trial metadata.

### Paper 10 — David H. Bailey and Marcos López de Prado (2014)

**Exact title:** *The Deflated Sharpe Ratio: Correcting for Selection Bias, Backtest Overfitting and Non-Normality*  
**Publication:** Journal of Portfolio Management, 40(5), 94–107.  
**DOI:** 10.3905/jpm.2014.40.5.094

**Concept:** Adjust the statistical significance of a Sharpe ratio for multiple trials, sample length, skewness, and kurtosis.

**Modeling belief/assumption:** A high backtest Sharpe after many experiments may be a statistical fluke, especially with non-normal FX returns.

**Core principle:** Compare the observed Sharpe to an inflated expected maximum Sharpe under multiple testing, then correct for non-normality and track length.

**EURUSD H1 implementation:** Report DSR for any proposed decision or session policy alongside raw Sharpe, drawdown, PBO, turnover, and sample count. Require DSR and walk-forward conditional accuracy before production influence.

**Benefit:** Stops research-grade additions from being approved solely because of an attractive raw performance ratio.

**Efficient implementation:** Compute after a Full Research run, not during Quick Fields 1–3.

## 3. Recommended quant architecture

1. **Protected base + shadow evidence.** Never overwrite Field 1 decisions or the Power BI central path. Store session/regime/conformal outputs beside them with explicit identity and evidence tier.
2. **One completed-H1 authority.** Validate and normalize OHLC once; all Fields 1–3 use the same latest completed candle and broker time.
3. **Immutable same-candle generation reuse.** If source signature, completed candle, run identity, and required caches match, return the frozen generation without incrementing generation ID.
4. **Daily Middle/Higher publication.** Evaluate 120/600-candle standards at broker 00:00; lock for 24 hours; show transition risk separately.
5. **Session conditioning with shrinkage.** Never use a session-specific correction without adequate settled samples. Shrink to global and cap by ATR.
6. **Adaptive intervals, not forced point-path changes.** When evidence is weak, widen uncertainty instead of pushing the central path.
7. **Conditional validation.** Measure performance separately by Asia/Sydney, London, overlap, and New York, plus regime and transition state.
8. **Research governance.** SPA/PBO/DSR and chronological walk-forward validation belong in Full Research mode only.
9. **Compact state.** Keep current cards/scalars and a small display page in `st.session_state`; retain full histories in DuckDB/SQLite.
10. **Lazy UI.** Render only the selected Lunch field. Copy controls serialize current scalars only and never copy history DataFrames.

## 7. Limitations

- No live broker/API performance benchmark was possible in the packaging environment, so no fixed speedup percentage is claimed.
- Session-conditioned improvement is not guaranteed. The additive path must earn trust through settled, conditional walk-forward evidence.
- A 600-H1-candle window is approximately 25 trading days, not 600 calendar days. The implementation follows the user's requested 25-day/H1 interpretation.
- Daily locking improves display stability but can delay publication of a true intraday structural break; transition-risk diagnostics are shown to make that trade-off explicit.
